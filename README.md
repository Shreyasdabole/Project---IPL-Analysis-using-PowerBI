I wrapped up a fun project: an end-to-end interactive Power BI dashboard analyzing the complete history of the Indian Premier League (IPL) from 2008 to 2025.

As an IT professional working with data daily, I wanted to build something that takes massive amounts of historical sports data and turns it into clean, easily digestible insights.

Here are some of the key KPIs and features I focused on:
🔹 Dynamic Filtering: A responsive slicer to instantly update all visuals based on the selected season (2008–2025).
🔹 High-Level Tournament Stats: Quick views on total matches, teams, venues, and overall batting milestones (centuries, half-centuries, and total boundaries).
🔹 Team Standings: Prominent highlights of the Champions and Runners-up, alongside a comprehensive, detailed Points Table.
🔹 Player Accolades: Individual spotlight cards tracking the Orange Cap (most runs) and Purple Cap (most wickets) holders, plus the top boundary hitters (4s and 6s) for the year.

Building this was a great exercise within Power BI, ensuring the dashboard is both informative and visually engaging for cricket fans.
